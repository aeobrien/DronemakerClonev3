## Component Details

### #4 — Behringer UM2

Core audio interface PCB, board rev PCB302082REVJ_01. Stripped from original enclosure, user-facing components desoldered and replaced with panel-mount equivalents. Pots (#11-13) cleanly removed and reattached with hookup wire. XLR/TRS jack and LEDs still to complete.

Full teardown documentation: `docs/audio-interface/`

### #5 — Neutrik Combo XLR/TRS Jack

Part selection not finalised: NCJ6FI-S vs NCJ9FI-S depends on pin compatibility with phantom power wiring. See audio interface open questions.

### #10 — Phantom Power Toggle Switch

Originally planned for panel-mount backplate. Decision changed: phantom power left permanently on, switch stays soldered to UM2 board. Component in hand but not being relocated.

### #14-15 — UM2 Replacement LEDs

Original UM2 LEDs were 3mm, housed in a dual-LED plastic component. Cannot be reused in original form. Replacements are individual 5mm LEDs in 5mm chrome panel-mount bezels. **Bezels arrived 2026-04-02.** Measured: body ~6.1-6.2mm, flange 7.8mm. Panel hole: **6.2mm**. Need to verify forward voltage/current of originals to confirm compatibility — likely not critical for indicator LEDs.

### #16 — Teensy 4.1

Ethernet variant with headers. The Ethernet jack (if present on this specific board) is unused — check clearance during dry-fit. Sits in 2×24 pin socket headers (HC-PM254-8.5H-1x24PZ, 8.5mm height) on the custom PCB.

### #17 — Bourns PEC16-4220F-S0024

20mm flatted D-shaft, 24 detents, 24 pulses/rev, with push switch. Mounting hole 9.5mm. Max panel thickness ~2.7mm with washer+nut (Hammond top panel is 1.63mm — fine).

Datasheet: https://eu.mouser.com/datasheet/3/40/1/pec16.pdf

### #18 — Adafruit 3350 RGB Pushbutton

16mm mounting hole, panel thickness 1-7mm. Common-anode RGB LED (6V rated, operates 3-6V). Switch and LED circuits isolated. Cathodes driven via 220Ω resistors from shift registers.

Product page: https://thepihut.com/products/rugged-metal-pushbutton-16mm-6v-rgb-momentary

### #25 — MIDI Controller PCB

Ordering from JLCPCB via DHL Express. 163.6 × 81.1mm, 2-layer, 1.6mm, 1oz copper, green solder mask, HASL finish. Gerbers in `hardware/midi-controller/gerber_to_order/`.

### #26 — Aluminium Control Knobs

Plain solid aluminium, no position indicator, 6mm round/splined bore. Note: Alpha 9mm pots (and PEC16 encoders) have 6.0mm shafts, NOT 6.35mm (1/4"). These knobs are listed for 6mm so should fit. Verify on arrival.

Source: https://www.ebay.co.uk/itm/386817735048

### #28 — TUK KUCC1820BPM (USB-C Panel Mount)

USB-C socket on panel side with 200mm USB-C plug pigtail on inside. Plugs directly into Pi 5 USB-C power port. Rated 5A / 100W / 20V max. All pins pass through (including CC for PD negotiation). Standard profile keystone mount — **panel cutout: 16.2 × 14.7mm** (standard keystone opening). Purchased from CPC (CN21767), £9.58 inc VAT.

### #29 — Neutrik NAUSB3-B (USB 3.0 Feedthrough)

USB-A on outside, USB-B on inside. Reversible insert (can swap A/B sides without tools). Standard Neutrik D-size cutout (24.0 × 19.2mm). USB 3.0 (5 Gbps). Connect inside USB-B to Pi USB-A port with standard USB-A to USB-B cable. Zinc diecast housing, nickel plated. Purchased from CPC (CN20592).

### #32 — InnoMaker HiFi DAC HAT

PCM5122 DAC (384kHz/32bit) + TPA6133A2 headphone amplifier, I2S connection to Pi GPIO. Already owned. Will provide second audio output for independent headphone monitoring. The TPA6133A2 drives headphones directly — no separate amp needed. Output wired through passive dual-gang pot (#38) to panel-mount TRS jack. Untested with JUCE dual-device output.

### #33 — Headphone Amplifier (NOT NEEDED)

Originally planned as a separate component. The InnoMaker DAC HAT (#32) includes a TPA6133A2 headphone amp IC. Adequate for 32–250Ω headphones. No separate amp required.

### #38 — Headphone Volume Pot

Spare dual A10K from #36 (Alpha 9mm, originally bought for UM2 replacement). Wired as passive voltage divider between TPA6133A2 headphone output and panel-mount TRS jack. **With 1KΩ resistor from wiper to ground on each gang (#44)**, the taper is reshaped to give a usable volume sweep across the full rotation. Without the resistor, most of the volume change is crammed into the last 20% of rotation with low-impedance headphones. Mounts on faceplate, right side, bottom position.

### #34-37 — Superseded Components

These were originally planned but are no longer needed for their original purpose:
- **#34 Perfboards:** Replaced by custom PCB (#25)
- **#35-37 Alpha replacement pots:** Superseded by decision to reuse original UM2 pots (#11-13)

May be useful as spares or for other projects.
